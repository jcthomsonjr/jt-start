The vision of J.C.R. Licklider

Contrary to the thoughts of computer experts of the time, Licklider realized the potential of a large computer network for disseminating information and sharing ideas. The idea that a computer might be more than a machine that does math was foreign to the computer industry. This fact makes it clear that creating the network for communication rather than data manipulation was a worthwhile cause; the industry would most likely not attempt such a project. It soon became evident that communications was the true benefit of the network (Hauben).
